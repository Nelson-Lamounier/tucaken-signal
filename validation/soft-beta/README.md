# Soft beta — middle path between formal interviews and public cold launch

**Purpose:** Get most of what the formal interview pack would have
provided, at ~10% of the coordination cost, while building the
early-adopter cohort that public-cold launches lack.

## What this is (and isn't)

- **Is:** A "use this for a week, send me one paragraph of feedback
  if you have any" ask to 10–15 engineers from existing relationships.
- **Isn't:** Formal scheduled interviews with a question set
  (that's the interview pack, deferred to v1.1).

## Who to reach out to

10–15 engineers from your network. Mix of:
- Career stages (a few juniors, a few mid, a few senior+)
- Roles (DevOps, frontend, backend, ML, mixed)
- AI-tool usage (heavy, occasional, rare)
- Relationship strength (close colleagues + looser acquaintances)

The goal is *coverage*, not *consensus*. 10–15 because you'll get
3–6 actual responses, which is the validation cohort.

## What to send

Use `outreach-template.md`. ~80 words, low-commitment, no formal
scheduling.

## How to capture responses

Each response → `responses/<initials>.md` using the brief template
in `responses/_template.md`.

## What to look for in responses

From the research substitution, the highest-value signals to extract:

| Signal | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| Unprompted screenshot / share | Viral hypothesis confirmed |
| "Can I run this against X?" unprompted | Strongest possible signal — they're imagining other uses |
| Specific suggestion they applied | Trust-to-action conversion working |
| Specific suggestion they dismissed | Framing miss or noise to fix |
| Emotional language ("this is useful" vs "this is clever") | Adoption vs single-screenshot share |
| "Felt like overhead" or "felt punitive" | Reframe the pillar that triggered it |

## Timing relative to launch

- Day 1–2: Send 10–15 messages
- Day 3–10: Responses trickle in (watch but don't poll)
- Day 7–10: Run technical fixes from earlier review in parallel
- Day 10–12: Publishing prep complete + at least 3 responses in
- Day 12+: Public launch

The cohort that responded becomes your first 5–10 GitHub Issue
filers / Twitter mentions / quotes for launch materials.

## Decision gate

After 7 days:
- 0–1 responses → either the message was wrong (revise + resend)
  OR your network isn't the right ICP (signal worth heeding)
- 2–4 responses → enough to ship; capture and read carefully
- 5+ responses → strong signal, write up a synthesis like the
  interview-pack `analysis.md`

## What this loses vs formal interviews

Honest list:
- No scheduled hour of focused conversation
- No live reaction observation
- No follow-up probing on hesitations
- Lower response rate (3–6 of 15) vs interviews (5 of 5)

Acceptable trades for a free standalone tool launching to a
known-friendly cohort.
