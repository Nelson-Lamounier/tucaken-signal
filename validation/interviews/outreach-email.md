# Outreach email — interview recruitment

Customize the bracketed bits. Default tone is direct + low-commitment.

---

**Subject:** 30 min — react to a portfolio tool I'm building?

Hey [name],

I've been building a small CLI/skill that analyzes GitHub repos and
gives engineers feedback on how their portfolio reads to a recruiter
in 2026 (when AI-generated content has eroded resume trust).

One pillar in the tool recommends engineers add an `AI_USAGE.md`
documenting which parts of their projects used AI assistance and what
they overrode. The framing is "transparent AI use beats hidden AI
use" — but I want to validate that framing with real engineers
before it ships at default weight.

Would you have 30 minutes to react to it? I have 6 short questions.
No prep needed — I'd send you a sample `AI_USAGE.md` mid-call and
ask for your honest first reaction. If the idea feels like compliance
overhead to you, that's the most useful answer I could get.

Slot picker: [Calendly / Cal.com / your scheduling link]

Or just reply with 2–3 times that work for you this week / next.

Thanks,
[your name]

---

## Notes for sending

- Don't oversell. The interview is most useful if the candidate
  arrives slightly skeptical, not primed to be supportive.
- Don't share the question set in advance — answers should be
  first-instinct, not rehearsed.
- DO share that they're one of 5; signals it's serious enough to
  prepare for but small enough to be a chat.
- Compensate if appropriate (small gift card, donation, etc.) —
  signals their time is valued even at 30 min.
